The Core of Your Chart

The Ten Day Masters

Of the eight characters in your BaZi chart, one is you — the heavenly stem of your day of birth. Everything else in the chart is read in relation to it. This page is a field guide to all ten.

What is a Day Master? In BaZi, your birth moment is written as four pillars — year, month, day, hour — each made of two characters. The upper character of the day pillar is called the Day Master (日干). It isn't one trait among many; it's the reference point. Whether another element in your chart acts as wealth, pressure, support, or expression depends entirely on its relationship to this one character.

There are ten Day Masters: five elements — Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water — each in a yang (expansive, direct) and yin (adaptive, precise) mode. Think of them less as fortune-telling categories and more as ten distinct operating systems: ten ways of processing the world, wanting things, and being under pressure.

One honest caveat: the Day Master is the core of the reading, not the whole reading. Two Jia Wood people with different charts can live very different lives — season of birth, supporting elements, and luck cycles shape how the core expresses itself. Take what follows as the center of the portrait, not the finished painting.

甲

Jia — Yang Wood

The Tall Tree · upward, principled, unbending
“The Pioneer”
甲
Jia
The colossal tree
from the Elun deck →

Jia is the towering tree: it grows in one direction — up. People with this Day Master tend to have a strong internal compass and visible integrity; they'd rather break than bend, and others sense it. They are natural first-movers who feel most alive when building something long-term: a career, an institution, a family, a body of work. Growth for its own sake isn't enough — it has to be growth with a trunk, a direction, a point.

Strengths

Principled leadershipLong-term visionReliabilityMoral courage

The Shadow Side

The tree's weakness is its stiffness. Under stress, Jia becomes rigid — unable to pivot, slow to admit a chosen path was wrong, prone to doubling down out of pride. Because they equate flexibility with weakness, they can stay too long in dying projects and relationships. The growth edge for Jia is learning that bending is not breaking.

In Love & Connection

Loyal, protective, and slow to open — Jia loves like it grows: gradually, then permanently. Partners get stability and honesty, but may have to push for spontaneity and for Jia to say what it feels rather than what it decided.

Work & Direction

Thrives where vision and integrity are rewarded: founding, leadership, law, education, architecture — any field where things are built to last. Suffocates under micromanagement and short-term churn.

Appearance & Presence

Traditionally: tall or long-limbed, with an upright, straight-backed posture — a longer face, prominent brow, and a steady, level gaze. Jia carries itself like something that grew, not something assembled.

Yi — Yin Wood

The Vine · adaptive, persuasive, quietly unstoppable
“The Diplomat”
乙
Yi

Yi is the vine, the orchid, the grass that survives the storm that snaps the tree. Where Jia grows by force of will, Yi grows by finding the way around: reading the room, borrowing support, turning obstacles into trellises. People with this Day Master are socially intelligent, aesthetically tuned, and far tougher than they look — their flexibility is their strength, and it is easy to underestimate until you notice they've reached the top of the wall.

Strengths

AdaptabilitySocial intelligencePersuasionQuiet resilience

The Shadow Side

The vine needs something to climb — and there lies the trap. Under stress, Yi can become dependent on stronger figures, conflict-avoidant to the point of self-erasure, or quietly manipulative when direct asking feels too risky. The growth edge is developing its own trunk: stating needs plainly and tolerating the discomfort of open disagreement.

In Love & Connection

Warm, attentive, and highly responsive to a partner's moods — sometimes too responsive. Yi flourishes with partners who offer steadiness without domination, and wilts around people who mistake its gentleness for endless supply.

Work & Direction

Excels in negotiation, design, counseling, marketing, medicine, diplomacy — anywhere nuance beats force. Yi does its best work embedded in networks rather than alone on a mountaintop.

Appearance & Presence

Traditionally: slender and flexible in build, with soft, graceful features and expressive eyes. Movements are fluid, dress sense quietly aesthetic — and Yi often looks younger than its years.

Bing — Yang Fire

The Sun · radiant, generous, impossible to ignore
“The Radiant”
丙
Bing
The blazing sun

Bing is the sun: it shines on everyone, without calculation, because shining is simply what it does. People with this Day Master carry natural warmth and visibility — they energize rooms, speak in headlines, and give praise and help freely. There's an innate optimism and a big-picture instinct; details bore them, but direction excites them. Like the sun, they don't chase attention so much as attract it by existing.

Strengths

CharismaGenerosityOptimismBig-picture instinct

The Shadow Side

The sun cannot see its own shadow. Bing under stress burns hot: impatience, dramatics, promises made in enthusiasm and abandoned in boredom. Its self-worth can become tied to being seen — dimming badly when ignored or outshone. The growth edge is learning that warmth still counts when nobody is watching, and that follow-through is the sincerest form of shine.

In Love & Connection

Openhearted, demonstrative, and fun — Bing loves loudly. It needs a partner who genuinely enjoys its light rather than competing with it, and it must learn that steady presence, not grand gestures, is what intimacy runs on.

Work & Direction

Built for the front of the room: leadership, media, sales, entertainment, teaching, evangelizing a vision. Pair Bing with a detail-minded team and it becomes formidable; leave it alone with a spreadsheet and it dims.

Appearance & Presence

Traditionally: a broad, open forehead and bright complexion; an expressive face that flushes with feeling, a big natural smile, an energetic stride. Bing is the person you notice when they enter.

Ding is the candle, the lantern, the hearth — fire that is aimed rather than broadcast. Where Bing lights the world, Ding lights the page in front of it, and what it illuminates, it illuminates completely. People with this Day Master are perceptive to an almost uncomfortable degree: they notice moods, subtext, and the one detail everyone else missed. Their warmth is quieter but more personal — a Ding person makes you feel like the only one in the room.

The Shadow Side

A flame this sensitive flickers. Ding under stress turns inward: brooding, overthinking, replaying conversations at 2 a.m., burning its own fuel as anxiety. It can also flare suddenly — the mild-mannered Ding temper surprises everyone precisely because it's rare. The growth edge is protecting the flame: boundaries, rest, and not taking custody of every emotion it detects in others.

In Love & Connection

Deep, loyal, and intensely attentive — Ding bonds one-to-one and dislikes shallow circulation. It needs a partner who values emotional depth and won't exploit its instinct to keep others warm at its own expense.

Work & Direction

Shines in depth work: research, psychology, writing, craft, strategy, mentorship, spiritual or healing professions. Ding is the specialist, the editor, the advisor whom leaders quietly depend on.

Appearance & Presence

Traditionally: finer features with striking, bright eyes — the flame shows in the gaze. Often a slighter frame, a refined manner, and a warm but slightly reserved expression.

Wu is the mountain: massive, dependable, unmoved by weather. People with this Day Master radiate stability — they are the ones others instinctively stand behind in a crisis. Trust, once given, is nearly geological; commitments are kept not out of duty but because keeping things is simply their nature. Wu absorbs pressure that would crack other people and often becomes the unofficial load-bearing wall of a family or team.

Strengths

The Shadow Side

Mountains don't move — even when they should. Wu under stress becomes immovable: stubborn, change-resistant, emotionally sealed. It absorbs and absorbs and calls it strength, until the pressure surfaces as exhaustion or sudden rupture. The growth edge is twofold: letting some things go through rather than into it, and remembering that asking for help is not erosion.

In Love & Connection

Utterly reliable and quietly devoted — Wu shows love through presence and provision rather than words. Partners get bedrock; what they may have to excavate for is vulnerability. Wu must learn that being known is different from being needed.

Work & Direction

Natural fit for stewardship at scale: operations, real estate, finance, public service, infrastructure, family enterprise. Wu is who you want holding the thing that must not fall.

Appearance & Presence

Traditionally: a solid, broad-framed build with a strong nose and a calm, weighty presence. Unhurried movements, and a face that is hard to read and easy to trust.

Ji is the garden soil — the earth that feeds, not the earth that towers. People with this Day Master are natural cultivators: of talent, of teams, of children, of any living thing entrusted to them. They are tolerant, practical, and unglamorously essential; things simply grow better near them. Ji has a rare capacity to accept people as they are while patiently improving the conditions around them, and it accumulates resources the way soil accumulates richness — slowly, invisibly, permanently.

Strengths

The Shadow Side

Soil takes in everything — including what poisons it. Ji under stress over-accommodates: absorbing others' problems, saying yes past capacity, worrying in circles, and feeling used but unable to name it. Because it works underground, it can go chronically unrecognized and quietly resent it. The growth edge is boundaries: a garden has a fence for a reason, and Ji's care is more powerful when it is chosen, not extracted.

In Love & Connection

Attentive, forgiving, and deeply loyal — Ji builds a home wherever it stands. It pairs best with partners who notice and replenish its giving, and it must resist confusing being useful with being loved.

Work & Direction

Excels wherever things or people are grown: education, HR, healthcare, agriculture, hospitality, community building, long-horizon investing. Ji is the manager whose team never wants to leave.

Appearance & Presence

Traditionally: softer, rounded features and an approachable, easy face; a medium build and a warm, settled voice. Ji has the look of someone easy to sit next to — which is how it gets everywhere.

“The Vanguard”

Geng is raw metal — the ore, the axe, the sword before polishing. People with this Day Master are direct to the point of bluntness, allergic to injustice, and strangely energized by adversity: Geng is the one Day Master traditionally said to improve under fire, the way steel improves in the forge. They cut through ambiguity, say the thing everyone is thinking, and act while others deliberate. Loyalty runs deep and is expressed in deeds, not speeches.

Strengths

DecisivenessSense of justiceResilience under fireExecutional force

The Shadow Side

A blade cuts both ways. Geng under stress becomes harsh: brutal candor without timing, judgment without mercy, and a combativeness that turns allies into opponents. It can also mistake endurance for virtue, staying in punishing situations because hardship feels like proof of worth. The growth edge is sheathing the blade — learning that gentleness is a precision tool, not a compromise.

In Love & Connection

Fiercely protective and honest to a fault — with Geng you always know where you stand, which is both the gift and the bruise. It needs a partner who doesn't flinch at directness and can show Geng that softness is safe.

Work & Direction

Built for hard problems and high stakes: surgery, military and law enforcement, litigation, engineering, turnarounds, competitive sport. Give Geng a worthy fight and stand back.

Appearance & Presence

Traditionally: strong bone structure, a square jaw, an athletic or sturdy frame. Direct eye contact, a firm handshake, and a voice that carries to the back of the room.

People with this Day Master have exquisite standards: for their work, their appearance, their words, their circle. Selective and devoted — Xin doesn't do casual well. It offers a partner elegance, attentiveness, and fierce behind-the-scenes loyalty, and it needs appreciation expressed specifically: Xin can tell a generic compliment from a true one at a hundred paces.

Work & Direction

Excels where precision is the product: medicine, design, finance, luxury and beauty industries, jewelry, writing and editing, quality-driven craft of any kind. Xin is the standard-setter.

Appearance & Presence

Ren is the ocean and the great river: vast, restless, connecting distant shores. People with this Day Master think in systems and horizons — they absorb information omnivorously, adapt to any container, and carry an entrepreneurial current that keeps them moving toward the next opening. Ren is socially fluid in a different way from Yi: not by climbing, but by flowing — comfortable with strangers, at ease in foreign contexts, natural at connecting people and ideas across boundaries.

In Love & Connection

Generous, exciting, and hard to pin — Ren needs room to move and a partner who is a fellow traveler, not an anchor dragging bottom. Its loyalty is real but expressed in motion; it must learn that showing up on schedule is also romance.

Work & Direction

Thrives in trade, logistics, travel, media, venture-building, international anything — roles with moving parts and open water. Cage Ren in routine and it evaporates.

Appearance & Presence

Traditionally: a fuller or fluid build with lively, roving eyes and an animated expression. Restless energy, expansive gestures — Ren is magnetic in motion and visibly bored at anchor.

Work & Direction

Appearance & Presence
